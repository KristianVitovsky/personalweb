Dear Students,


on this short website there is the opportunity to share materials and help us orgaize the tutorials.
  • You will find all the homework sheets, lecture notes & organisational instructions from Prof. Dr. Ralf Klessen in the Uebungen system
  • We meet on Tuesdays at Philosophenweg 12 in room 056 from 9:15 to 11:00.
  • We will be working in a two week cycle: after the homework problem is uploaded to the uebungen system, you will have one week to work on it (it has to be uploaded on Wednesday the following week). I will then correct your solutions and upload the corrections to the system. The problem will then be discussed in the following tutorial.
  • You need to obtain at least 60% of the total points from the HW to be admitted to the exam.
  • You also have the obligation to present twice one of the exercise problems during the tutorial sessions. See below how this is organised.
  • Solutions to the sheets will not be uploaded, yet they will be discussed in the tutorials.
  • An attendance sheet will not be kept, yet it is expected that you attend the tutorials most of the time.
  • Please use my work email (kristian.vitovsky@h-its.org) to reach out if you have any questions. (I do not check other communication channels regularly.)




Presenting in the tutorials

Under this LINK you will find a google document, here you can volunteer to present in the next tutorial and note questions you would like to discuss.

  • Presenting usualy means the full exercise from the HW sheet.
  • If nobody volunteers, I will pick someone without mercy.
  • You have to do these at least twice in the semester.
  • Ask questions!
  • At the point of presenting you will also have the corrections at hand, so please incorporate them into your presentation.




What will we try in the upcoming sessions?

  • First 10 - 15 minutes will be open for questions about the HW sheet you will be working on in that week.
  • Next, we will have a brief recap of the key concepts of the theory connect to the HW that is to be discussed that week.
  • The rest of the tutorials will be dedicated to the presentations of HW exercises.
